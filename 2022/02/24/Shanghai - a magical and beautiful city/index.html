Shanghai - a magical and beautiful city

 · 2022-2-24 · 次阅读

Shanghai is really beautiful, the beauty is intoxicating!

If it is the first time to travel to Shanghai,

it is estimated that no one will not go to the Bund,

The Bund is indeed the most representative

and irreplaceable scenic spot in Shanghai, no one.

I recommend everyone to go.

This is a city that embraces all rivers, with a mix of old and new buildings.

The future and the retro coexist, often seen and always new, unique

In the long river of time, the civilizations created by human beings

are constantly changing，

from birth to prosperity to decline and then to demise.

This is the fate of civilization, so those relics of human civilization

that were once gorgeous

but have weakened or even disappeared today,

Especially well-preserved relics and intangible cultural heritage

are more worthy of our visit in our limited life.

There is a rich cultural life here, large exhibitions and small exhibitions,

Art Exhibition Architecture Exhibition History Exhibition,

every weekend is different

Three-dimensional space limits the width of our life,

one-dimensional linear time limits the length of our life,

So in my opinion, the greatest meaning of travel,

It lies in transcending the limitations of time and space

and experiencing a different life.

Away from the hustle and bustle of traffic,

Stroll along the newly renovated 45km riverside,

Enjoy the most beautiful scenery of Huangpu River.

Get on board at Shiliupu Pier and drive north to Yangpu Bridge.

Drive south to Nanpu or Lupu Bridge, then turn back to Shiliupu Pier.

The night cruise on the Pujiang River is really good!

I really can’t think of a city with a more beautiful night view of the river

than the Huangpu River, including Hong Kong’s Victoria Bay.

Shanghai is not just reinforced concrete and fast-paced,

There are also small forests in the city,

and occasionally the warm wind blows,

Bring two or three friends, or go out or have a picnic,

I just want to have a free time without distractions.

Strolling in the alleys of Tianzifang,

I saw a small shop dressed in a variety of expressions,

There are traditional Shanghai cheongsam, cosmetics, paper fan umbrellas, paper-cuts……

There are also gourmet and cultural

and creative product storefronts from all over the world.

on the bar seats,

Sitting with tourists from all over the world,

leisurely experience the rare taste of slow life here.

There are unique cultural trinkets everywhere beside

the walls and corner steps,

showing the unique Shanghai-style cultural charm of Tianzifang

in every square inch.

I see the morning light rising little by little on the horizon,

outlining the building,

I want to indulge in it and don’t want the passage of time!

I did not wait until the sun was completely high,

but riding this slightly warm light

Start taking pictures of your surroundings.

The rising sun is already rising in the east,

and looking back to the west,

it seems that it is still in the silent night.

Go east to the Waibaidu Bridge,

Here you can also explore some angles to shoot Lujiazui.

I love the sloping light in the morning,

which brings some gentle texture to the picture.

At sunset, go back and enjoy the sun shining

brightly behind the clouds above the buildings on the Bund

until it becomes gentle.

And then disappear little by little!

Shanghai is beautiful！

The beauty is mesmerizing!
