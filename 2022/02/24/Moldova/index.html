Moldova - a unique European travel experience!

Posted on 2022-02-24 Edited on 2022-02-14 Views:

If you are tired of staying in the city all day,

looking up are all high-rise buildings;

I was repressed everywhere between life and work,

but I couldn’t shout loudly to vent the repression in my heart.

If you want to escape the hustle and bustle of the city,

find a quiet place where the restless heart can stay.

So, go to Moldova! ! !

As a traditional agricultural country,

Moldova attracts tourists with its natural and simple rural style.

Moreover, the local natural environment has never been polluted,

and one can feel the harmony and unity between man and nature.

Here you can escape from the hustle and bustle of the city

and let vacationers from afar enjoy Moldova to the fullest.

The ease of life in the countryside.

If you catch up with local festivals,

you can also sing, dance and have a good time with

the warm and friendly Moldovan people.

In addition to escaping the hustle and bustle of the city,

visit Moldova’s tourist attractions.

For example: you can go to the Orheiul Vechi cave monastery

overlooking the Raut river,

Or go to Curchi Monastery, an Orthodox church with religious symbols

and impressive paintings…

So, don’t hesitate to go to Moldova to experience it!

Wine tasting!

If you are a wine lover, then be sure to visit the local wine tasting

Moldova has a unique geographical environment

that can breed the best quality wines.

It has more than 5,000 years of winemaking history

and is one of the earliest countries in the world to make wine.

One of the most prestigious wineries, purcari,

has made the Queen of England choose wine here for five consecutive years.

The wine of this winery is the designated table wine

for some high-end people in Europe.

If you are in the local area, you can taste the unique taste of local wine

at a relatively favorable price!

If you want to feel the rich local wine culture,

it is best to visit the first weekend in October,

Come to Moldova for this wine festival!

The festival celebrates Moldova’s rich winemaking tradition.

During the festival, the winery will be very confident

and proud to show the carefully brewed wine to the guests who come to attend,

And provide tourists with the opportunity to taste for free.

In addition, several local restaurants and cafés serve traditional delicacies:

grilled meats, placinta (Moldovan cheese-filled pie),

other national delicacies, fruits and desserts.

If you want to experience a different Christmas, then go to Moldova!

Moldova celebrates Christmas from December 24th to 26th,

and preparations begin in November,

It included baking cakes and slaughtering pigs,

followed by three days of banquets and exchange of gifts interestingly.

Moldova celebrates Christmas twice a year:

Orthodox and Catholics follow different calendars,

The Catholic Church celebrates Christmas on December 25th, and the Orthodox Church celebrates Christmas on January 7th.

Most Moldovans believe in the Orthodox Church,

and Christmas on January 7 is relatively lively.

Christmas is the most romantic and charming time in Moldova.

Streets and shops are lit up and decorated,

thousands of festive lights come on one by one

The whole city is brightly lit, gleaming under the bright stars, creating a beautiful festive atmosphere.

The Christmas market dates back to the Middle Ages,

with hundreds of idyllic Christmas wooden houses

spread out along the sidewalks towards the square

Scattered throughout the city center,

a fairytale-like winter wonderland;

It attracts tourists who come and go and enjoy a variety of happiness

in the world of ice and snow.

At the Christmas market,

people can experience the oldest and purest traditions of Moldova,

In the hut, there are various Christmas-themed dolls,

china, garlands, handmade ornaments,

and Christmas delicacies such as gingerbread, sausages, and desserts.
